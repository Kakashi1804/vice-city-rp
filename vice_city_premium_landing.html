Example system flow — drug economy
Continuous player-driven loop · No admin scripting required · Self-sustaining conflict engine
The loop
This is not a storyline an admin writes. It is a system that runs automatically, reacts to player behavior, and generates conflict, alliances, and content on its own. Every step below fires because a player made a choice.
1
Import phase — Cartel receives supply
Admin-triggered or fired automatically based on dock control data. Cartel sets the global drug rate at point of import. The rest of the server economy moves in response — whether players know it or not.
Who acts: Cartel leadership · Admin reads dock control log · Probability table fires automatically
Cartel controls docks → arrives intact Kings control docks → 40% theft chance Smugglers control → 70% theft chance VCPD controls → seized → war arc fires
2
Distribution phase — gangs purchase and distribute
Overtown Kings buy supply from Cartel at the current rate. Distribute to street corners. Their margin is street price minus Cartel rate. Cartel can raise or drop that rate at any time — every change creates pressure Kings have to react to.
Who acts: Kings buy · Cartel prices · Players don't see the formula — they feel the pressure
Price raised → Kings absorb, raise, or bypass Price dropped → Kings territory devalues Bypass attempt → conflict event fires
3
Disruption phase — supply chain can be intercepted
Any faction can break the chain. Smugglers accept contracts to intercept. VCPD seizes at point of sale. Kings rob a handoff. Syndicate routes a staged seizure through a bribed commander. Every disruption creates a downstream consequence that ripples through the entire economy.
Who acts: Any faction · Disruptions are strategic choices with calculated risk/reward
Smuggler intercept → black market opens 48h VCPD seize → scarcity fires → street price doubles Syndicate staged → product routes to laundering
4
Law enforcement response — VCPD reacts to activity level
VCPD doesn't act randomly. A live visibility score accumulates based on how much noise factions have made. When it crosses a threshold, the response tier escalates automatically. The Syndicate's most valuable service is suppressing this score — buying quiet for every faction that can afford it.
Who acts: VCPD responds to score · Syndicate can suppress · Factions generate or reduce visibility
VISIBILITY += drug_sale(×1) + conflict(×3) + failed_transport(×2)
VISIBILITY -= bribe_paid(×4)

score < 30 → VCPD passive
score 30–60 → VCPD active raids
score 60+ → full crackdown event fires
