Week 1 — establish
What happensFactions come online and begin claiming territory, making deals, and recruiting.
Admin roleMonitor faction balance. Deploy "Hot Intel" to any faction that's slow to engage.
Content momentFirst contact between factions. Pure raw energy. No script. Great stream bait.
Week 2 — trigger fires
What happensThe conditional trigger system reads faction control data and fires the mid-point event automatically.
Admin roleWatch the 48h player window. If no resolution, force-escalate to the next branch.
Content momentParanoia spreads. Every faction suspects another. Cross-faction deals explode.
Week 3 — alliances break
What happensThe mid-season twist activates. An NPC or staff-run character drops new information that changes everything.
Admin roleRun the twist NPC. Stay in character. Brief only the directly affected faction lead 24h ahead.
Content momentThe double-cross window. Best drama of the season. Every creator wants to be online this week.
Week 4 — finale
What happensServer-wide event. All factions online. Outcome is calculated from season-long activity data.
Admin roleRun the event script. Calculate outcome privately. Announce result. Tease Season 2 immediately.
Content momentMaximum stakes. Mandatory stream week. Every creator posts. Server gets its biggest audience of the season.

Admin control panel
You run the world — the system handles the story
Trigger system — events react to players, not scripts
Admins set conditions. Players determine which branch fires. You never have to force a story — the logic does it.
IF Cartel controls docks → shipment arrives safely → no event
IF Kings control docks → 40% theft probability per run
IF Smugglers control docks → 70% theft probability (motive)
IF VCPD controls docks → shipment seized → Cartel-VCPD war arc

→ Stolen: open 48h investigation window
→ No resolution by 48h: force-escalate, random faction accused
→ False evidence planted: planter becomes suspect if discovered
Admin rule: never tell players the formula. They should feel like their choices mattered — not like they gamed a system.
Faction influence tracking
TerritoryEach faction controls named nodes on the map. Admin updates control status every 72h based on activity logs.
StandingPublic leaderboard updated every 3 days. Shows season objectives and current progress per faction.
Loyalty logAdmin-side record of every deal, betrayal, and defection. Referenced at finale to determine outcomes.
Balance checkIf any faction drops below 5 active members, "New Blood" event fires within 24h automatically.
Outcome control
Branch limitMax 3 outcome branches per event. More than 3 is unmanageable live.
48h ruleEvery open trigger has a 48h player window before admin escalation fires.
Finale scoreCalculated privately from territory + activity + key decisions. Announced only as a result — never the math.
Paper trailEvery trigger activation logged in a private admin channel with timestamp and which branch fired.
Admin roles (minimum viable staff)
Head AdminOwns the season narrative. Final call on all trigger outcomes. Plays the Don NPC in Season 5.
Faction LeadsOne mod embedded in each faction. Monitors RP quality. Reports griefing without breaking immersion.
Event OpsRuns trial and election logistics. Never in a player faction. Permanently neutral.
Content LeadSingle point of contact for streamers. Sends 48h alerts before major events. Coordinates clip compilations.
Lore KeeperMaintains the server wiki, NPC dossiers, and event history. Owns continuity across seasons.
Total staffMinimum 5 people. Can overlap roles on a small server. Never let one person run everything — burnout kills servers.
Crisis playbook
Faction collapseDrops below 5 members mid-season → fire "New Blood" immediately. Never let a faction die quietly.
PowergamingOne in-character warning via faction lead. If repeated → faction-administered consequence (demotion, exile). Avoid public admin announcements.
OOC dramaMandatory 24h cooling-off. Private mediation before any public action. Public bans are a last resort.
Dead seasonActivity under 60% of Season 1 baseline by Day 20 → compress finale to Day 25. Energy over completion.
Faction architecture
Five factions · Three power tiers · One ecosystem that forces interaction
The hard rule
No faction can win a season without making at least one deal with an enemy faction. The dependency web is not flavor — it is mechanical.
Tier 1Drug Supply
Leonida Cartel
MechanicSets the server's global drug price. Every cent the Kings earn flows through a Cartel-controlled rate.
Chain effectPrice raised → Kings' margins compress → Kings attempt bypass → conflict event triggers.
Win stateControl 3+ distribution nodes and survive the succession arc.
Content hookInternal betrayal, loyalty tests, political succession, Game of Thrones energy.
Tier 2Street Control
Overtown Kings
MechanicLast-mile distribution. They move product the Cartel can't physically reach. Price takers, not price setters.
Chain effectKings' street sales fund Smuggler weapons buys. If Kings are broke, Smugglers starve too.
Win stateControl 5+ street corners and negotiate a direct Smuggler arms deal.
Content hookCome-up arcs, turf war footage, loyalty drama. Highest action-per-session faction.
Tier 1Finance
The Syndicate
MechanicAll dirty money must pass through Syndicate laundering. No clean money moves on the server without their cut.
Chain effectSyndicate controls political donations → influences VCPD budget → shapes how aggressively law enforcement operates.
Win statePlace a bribed political candidate and control 2 VCPD commanders.
Content hookSlow-burn corruption arcs, political intrigue. Built for creators who want character-driven storytelling.
Tier 2Weapons
Los Cayos Smugglers
MechanicSole weapons supplier on the server. No faction can arm up without going through them. Pure leverage.
Chain effectSmugglers embargo a faction → that faction can't escalate conflicts → power balance shifts within days.
Win stateComplete 10 supply runs without Coast Guard interception this season.
Content hookHeists, double-crosses, open water chases. Mercenary energy — they work for whoever pays.
Tier 1Law
Vice City PD
MechanicCan freeze assets, issue warrants, and impound vehicles. The ultimate check on any faction that over-extends.
Chain effectSyndicate bribes VCPD → enforcement drops → Cartel operates freely → Kings push harder → city destabilizes.
Win stateMaintain public approval above 40% OR fully corrupt the force — two completely different gameplay paths.
Content hookDirty cop arcs are the most-watched RP content category on the internet. This faction is a content machine.

Season 4 — Internal Affairs
VCPD's season · Corruption trial · Player-run IA detective
▼
IA DetectiveTrusted community member selected by staff. A real player. Can be bribed, manipulated, or turned.
Mid-seasonEvidence surfaces linking Syndicate directly to 3 VCPD commanders. Question isn't if corruption exists — it's how high it goes.
FinalePublic tribunal. Corrupt officers permanently retired unless they can escape the city before the verdict.
Season 5 — The Succession War
The Don is dying · The will · Coronation or assassination
▼
Central NPCThe Don — staff-run, referenced since S1. Terminal illness announced Day 1. The whole server has been building toward this.
Mid-seasonThe will names a civilian character nobody expected. Everyone wants them dead or controlled.
Final 2 weeksDaily activity tracked. Every faction is influencing the outcome in real time. Whoever controls the throne reshapes S6.
Season 6 — Hurricane Season
Category 5 incoming · Hidden infrastructure revealed · Reconstruction war
▼
Setup72h in-world warning. Each faction reacts according to their nature — Syndicate moves assets, Kings refuse to leave their corners, Smugglers charge for evacuation.
Mid-seasonStorm uncovers hidden tunnels, safe houses, and buried money. Territory map partially resets.
FinaleReconstruction race. Factions compete for rebuild contracts and the power vacuum left by those who fled and never came back.
This season is a content goldmine. Storm-hit Vice City is visually unlike anything on the server. Brief creators before it starts — they will want to film everything.

People don't donate to keep a server online. They donate to protect a world they're emotionally invested in. This system builds that investment systematically.
What drives donations specifically
Rank protectionPlayers who've earned Capo or Detective donate to stay active. Rank is worth something real here.
Finale stakesPlayers who are 3 weeks deep into a season will not miss the finale. They show up, they're active, they give.
Community identityThe Vice City Herald, lore posts, and named NPCs make players feel they belong to something. Belonging is worth paying for.
Content investmentStreamers who built a channel on your server have career skin in the game. They evangelize and they donate.
Revenue-safe design rules
No pay-to-winEvery progression gate is activity-based, not purchase-based. Donors get cosmetic and lore perks only.
Creator perksPartner and Featured creator tiers are free. Built on content output, not payment. This keeps quality streamers on the server without resentment.
Longevity6 seasons of storyline = roughly 18+ months of structured content before the world needs to evolve. Long shelf life per investment.
ReactivationEvery returning player gets a re-entry mission. Churned players can come back. Churned players who come back donate at higher rates.
The servers that make consistent money are not the ones with the most players. They are the ones with players who feel like they would lose something if the server shut down. This system builds that feeling on purpose.
