Trigger System
Player behavior drives events — not admin fiat
Philosophy
Events are not stories you write in advance. They are conditional outcomes that fire based on what players actually do. Admins set the conditions. Players determine which branch activates.
S1 Trigger: The Missing Shipment
Phase 1Track which faction controls the docks node at week 2.
IF Cartel controls docks → shipment arrives safely → no event
IF Kings control docks → 40% theft probability on each run
IF Smugglers control docks → 70% theft probability (they have motive)
IF VCPD controls docks → shipment seized → Cartel-VCPD war arc triggers
Phase 2Shipment stolen → 48h investigation window opens. Factions can submit evidence, plant evidence, or ignore it.
IF evidence submitted by 48h → culprit revealed → trial event
IF no resolution by 48h → admins force-escalate: random faction accused
IF player submits false evidence → they become suspect if caught
Outcome ACorrect culprit found → public trial, server votes on punishment
Outcome BWrong faction blamed → wrongful conviction arc, revenge subplot S2
Outcome CNobody found → shipment resurfaces mid-S2 in enemy hands
S2 Trigger: The Election
ELECTION_SCORE = (territory_points × 2) + (bribes_paid × 1.5) + (evidence_destroyed × 3) - (evidence_exposed × 4)

Highest score faction → their candidate wins
Winning faction gets: +20% laundering rate OR warrant immunity for 2 weeks
Admin note: Calculate score privately. Announce only the winner. Never reveal the formula publicly — players should feel like their choices mattered without gaming the math.
Universal Trigger Rules
48h ruleEvery open trigger has a 48-hour player window before admin escalation. Players who miss the window lose narrative control.
Visible stakesAlways announce what's at stake before the trigger fires. Players make better choices when they understand consequences.
Branch limitMaximum 3 outcome branches per trigger. More than 3 becomes unmanageable.
Paper trailLog every trigger activation in a private admin channel with timestamp and which branch fired.

Daily jobEvery rank has one repeatable faction job available per 24h. Takes 15–20 minutes. Earns rank XP.
Faction bulletinPosted each morning by a faction leader (player or staff). 3 optional missions with different risk/reward profiles.

Cross-Faction Interaction Points
These exist specifically to force players out of their faction bubble — the source of the best RP content.
The DealEvery faction has one "open deal slot" per week — any other faction can request a meeting. Accepting earns both sides XP. Refusing is logged.
The RatPlayers can defect and feed intel to an enemy faction. Risk: exposure. Reward: rank skip + new identity. Limit: once per season per player.
The NeutralCivilians and freelancers can complete jobs for any faction without joining. Permanent independent track with its own progression.

Event Scripts
Execution blueprints — not just descriptions
The Public Trial
Required for: S1 Finale · S4 Tribunal · Any major conviction event
Judge1 staff member or senior community member. Controls pacing. Final say on procedural disputes. Cannot vote on outcome.
Jury7 players selected by random draw from active members. Must not be in the accused faction. 72h advance notice required.
Prosecution2 players from the opposing faction OR civilian players. Submit evidence 24h before trial via designated Discord channel.
DefenseAccused player + 1 chosen ally. Gets 24h to review prosecution evidence and prepare a counter.
PublicAll other players are observers. May submit 1 written statement each to the Judge before proceedings begin.
TimelineOpening statements (10 min each) → Evidence review (30 min) → Cross-examination (20 min) → Closing (10 min each) → Jury deliberation (20 min private) → Verdict
Possible verdicts
Guilty
Conviction
Character imprisoned or exiled. Player can appeal once. Must sit out 2 weeks of active RP.
Not guilty
Acquittal
Accusing faction loses credibility. Faction reputation drops publicly for S2.
Mistrial
Hung jury
48h recess. New jury drawn. Tension doubles. This is the most dramatic outcome — let it breathe.
Black Friday — 48h Chaos Event
TriggerBetween seasons. Announced 48h in advance.
RuleEvery contract, hit, and deal is worth double. No faction truces enforced. All bets off.
Admin roleOne admin online per 8-hour window. No intervention unless real-life rules are broken. Let chaos play out.
Content pushAlert all connected streamers 24h in advance. This is a recording event. Clip compilations after.
Hard stop48h exactly. Server announcement when window closes. Contracts from the event pay out 1h after close.
The Rat — Paranoia Event
SetupAnonymous tip sent to VCPD names one high-ranking member of every faction as an informant. None confirmed true.
Duration2 weeks. Cannot be cut short. Paranoia must have time to compound.
Admin ruleStaff knows which tips are true (some are, some aren't). Never reveal this. Let players investigate.
ResolutionAt end of 2 weeks, staff reveals which tips were real and which were planted. Wrong accusations have consequences.
The best outcome is when players accuse someone innocent. Real drama. Handle with care — debrief the accused player before public reveal.

Chaos Control — When Things Go Wrong
PowergamingIf a player is breaking immersion by refusing to accept consequences: one warning, then faction lead delivers an in-character consequence (demotion, exile). Never break immersion with a public admin announcement if you can avoid it.
Faction collapseIf a faction loses half its players mid-season: deploy the "New Blood" standalone event immediately. Never let a faction die silently — it kills server energy.
Drama bleedOOC conflict between players about IC events: mandatory 24h cooling-off period. If unresolved, head admin mediates privately before any public action. Public bans are a last resort.
Dead seasonIf player activity drops below 60% of season 1 baseline by Day 20: compress the finale to Day 25. Cut a subplot. Keep energy high rather than dragging a weak arc to Day 30.
Retention System
Why players stay · What keeps them coming back
The Three Retention Pillars
InvestmentPlayers must have something to lose. Ranks, assets, reputation — all are at risk each season. The fear of losing keeps people logging in.
AnticipationSomething significant should happen every 72 hours on the server. Not a finale — just a moment. A deal, a rumor, a new development. Players check in because they fear missing something.
IdentityPlayers need to feel their character matters to the story. Mention player characters in server lore updates. Reference past decisions in new events. People stay for worlds where they exist.

Community Growth Strategy
Organic flywheel · Referral loops · Quality control
The Recruitment Flywheel
Content gets posted → New viewer sees it → Viewer applies → Player joins → Player creates content → Repeat
Every system on this server should accelerate one step of this loop. If it doesn't — question why it exists.
Onboarding — First 72 Hours
The first 72 hours determines whether a new player stays. Build this ritual:
Hour 1
Welcome DM from staff. Two sentences. Human, not automated. Link to faction guide only — no info dump.
Hour 6
New player assigned a faction sponsor (existing member). Sponsor shows them one thing that day only.
Hour 24
New player completes their first job. Small stakes. Guaranteed success. Builds confidence.
Hour 48
New player introduced to faction in a group session. Name said out loud by faction lead. They exist now.
Hour 72
Check-in DM from sponsor. One question: "What's one thing you want to do in Vice City?" That becomes their first personal arc hook.
Referral System
MechanicEvery player gets a unique referral code. When a recruit uses it and stays active for 2 weeks, the referrer gets a faction benefit (small XP bonus, cosmetic reward).
Quality gateReferred players still go through standard application. The referral code skips the waitlist, not the vetting process.
CapMax 3 active referral bonuses per season per player. Prevents spam recruitment of low-quality members.
Quality Control — Vibe Protection
The server's reputation is its most valuable asset. Protect it harder than anything else.
Application2 questions max. "What kind of RP do you want to create?" and "Tell us about a character you've played." Filters for people who think about storytelling.
Trial periodFirst 2 weeks: associate rank only. No access to major events. Faction sponsor reports on their conduct privately.
Culture standardOne public post from staff per season defining what "good RP" looks like on this server with a real example. Standards are shown, not just stated.
Longevity & Replayability
What happens after Season 6 · How new players enter mid-story · How the world resets without erasing history
The Living World Model
The server doesn't reset — it evolves. Season 7+ is built on the consequences of Seasons 1–6. Players who were here for Season 1 have a world that shows the marks of their decisions.
Legacy systemRetired characters become NPCs. Their names appear in lore. New players can reference them. The history is real.
New factionsSeason 7+ can introduce new factions based on what the story created — Santo Peligro remnants, a IA detective who went rogue, the civilian who inherited the Cartel.
Power shiftsFaction standings from each finale carry forward. Nobody starts fresh. The world remembers.
Mid-Season Entry System
New players shouldn't feel like they missed the show. Give them a reason to care about joining mid-story.
Entry packageNew players get a 2-paragraph "previously on Vice City" briefing written in character. Not a wiki link — an actual story summary that makes them feel like they're catching up on a show.
Fresh character hookEvery new mid-season character starts with one unresolved loose thread from the current season (given by staff). They have a reason to investigate. They matter immediately.
CapNo new players during the final 7 days of a season. Protect the finale experience for people who've been there.
Season 7+ Generation System
After Season 6, the server community votes on three things that shape Season 7:
Vote 1Which faction should have a "redemption arc" — their standing is worst, they get a story centered on comeback
Vote 2Which past event should have lasting consequences that haven't been addressed yet
Vote 3Which player character (retired or active) should become a named NPC in the world going forward
This vote is the most powerful retention tool you have. Players who shape Season 7 will not miss Season 7.
The Meta-Rule — Always
Player actions must create permanent marks on the world.
If the ending is the same regardless of what players do — they're NPCs in their own story.

Every finale must have at least two real, mechanically distinct endings. Build the branch. Trust the players to fill it.
